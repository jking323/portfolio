Every build starts the same way: with a vehicle that runs fine and a person
who decides it shouldn't stay that way. The donor is a Jeep — solid frame,
honest rust, the kind of bones worth building on. The goal is a dirt rod: a
rat-rod stance and attitude on a chassis that can actually take a beating off
pavement.

{{< figure src="/img/dirt-rod/donor-jeep.jpg" caption="The donor, the day it rolled into the shop." >}}

## The plan

- Strip it to the frame and assess what's actually solid
- Keep the inline-six — torque over horsepower, and it sounds right
- Drop the stance, widen the track, leave the patina
- Build it to run dirt, not to win a car show

## Teardown

First weekend was all teardown. Body panels off, interior gutted, everything
labeled and bagged. The frame is in better shape than expected — surface rust,
nothing structural.
